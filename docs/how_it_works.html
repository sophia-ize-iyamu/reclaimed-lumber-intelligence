The pipeline, end to end

Five stages. Each box on the left feeds the next, and every coefficient in the model carries a citation and a plausible range.

01

Sourcing & inputs

  • Toronto Open Data (live demolition permits, daily)
  • StatCan 2021 Census (dwellings, housing age)
  • StatCan Building Permits (demolition rates)
  • Coefficients (USDA FPL, Oregon DEQ, MPAC)
02

Canonical table

  • One agreed schema for every city
  • Void / coverage flags
  • Marks real versus modelled
03

Recovery cascade

  • Framing lumber in the building
  • Recoverable, then salvageable
  • Spec-ready, then dollar value
  • One sourced coefficient per step
04

Uncertainty

  • Monte Carlo over every range
  • P10 / P50 / P90 bands
  • Tornado: the lever that matters
05

Outputs

  • 5 to 10 year forecast
  • Maps and gap analysis
  • Value and scenarios
  • Platform roadmap

Coverage tier sets the confidence band

The tier each market lands in decides how wide its Monte Carlo band is. That is the void analysis made quantitative.

CoverageWhich marketsBand
HIGHToronto (live permit feed)+/- 10%
MEDIUMVancouver, Montreal, Calgary, Edmonton, Ottawa-Gatineau (real StatCan figures)+/- 25%
LOWThe other 19 CMAs (inferred from dwellings and rate)+/- 45%

The band on the demolition count combines with the sampled coefficient ranges in the Monte Carlo, so low-data cities get an honestly wide P10 to P90 spread rather than false precision.
